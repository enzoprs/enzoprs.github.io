Rallye

Rallying is an off-road motor race in which drivers cover a variety of challenging roads, including gravel and tarmac. Competitions take place over several stages, called special stages, testing the speed, precision and strategy of the participants. It's a thrilling fusion of power, technique and navigation

Single-seater

Formula 1 (F1) represents the pinnacle of motorsport, bringing together the most talented drivers and the most prestigious teams in a global competition. Further down, we find Formula Two (F2) and Formula Three (F3), two levels of racing that are often the stepping stones to F1. Further down, Formula 4 (F4) is often seen as the gateway to young talent. Each category has its own level of intensity, speed and competition, creating a promotional pathway for budding drivers to reach the heights of Formula 1.

GT

GT (Gran Turismo) racing uses modified sports cars designed specifically for the road to race on the track. These events, like endurance races, demand speed and durability from both car and team.
